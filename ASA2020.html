The Relationship of Variability to Contrast When Controlling For Distance Between Category Means

Both the amount of variability in the distribution of each labeled category and the distance between category means/the distance between the distributions is likely to affect the fitting of a logistic regression model to data. There was some concern that the relationship between variability and contrast shown in this analysis could be confounded if there was a relationship between variability and the distance between category means.

To determine whether this confound was a potential issue in the analysis, for each dimension in each segment pair, the distance between the category means was measured and compared to a pooled variability measurement for the segments in the pair (calculated by centering the data for each segment, combining the centered data, and calculating the IQR of the combined data)

The results of this analysis for the articulatory data (analysis of the acoustic data still to be completed) are given in the table below ('COR' in cell = significant correlation between IQR and mean difference for that dimension in that segment comparison). For the majority of the measured dimensions, the calculated IQR values were not correlated with the distance between means.

To further determine the extent to which the relationship between variability and regression coefficients exists independent of the distance between means, multiple regression models were fit with the distance between means and IQR as predictors and the logistic regression coefficients as the response variable.
