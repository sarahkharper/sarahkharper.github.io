About me

I am a third year PhD student in Linguistics at the University of Southern California. My research explores speech production and articulation, specifically articulatory-acoustic relations, articulatory variability, and the development of articulatory patterns in second language acquisition.

At USC, I am a member of the Speech Articulation and Knowledge Group (SPAN), the USC Phonetics and Phonology group, and the Zevin Lab.

In my spare time, I enjoy ballroom dancing, and compete at the collegiate level with the SC Ballroom and Latin Dance Team.
