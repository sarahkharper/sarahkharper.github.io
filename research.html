Current Projects

Articulatory-Acoustic Relations and Stylistic Variation in /r/ Production

Relevant Citations: Harper, Goldstein, & Narayanan, 2017 (PaPE Poster), In Preparation.

Broad Aims:

  • Investigate individual acoustic contributions of the three supralaryngeal constriction gestures involved in the production of American English /r/
  • Explore how these articulatory-acoustic relationships may vary under different speaking styles.

My first screening paper focused on modeling the relationship between F3 values and aperture for the labial, palatal, and pharyngeal constrictions in /r/ production. Using rtMRI data from the USC TIMIT Corpus , we found that individual speakers differ in the relative contribution of the palatal and pharyngeal constriction gestures to the acoustic signal, with the relative importance of each to F3 lowering dependent on the location and length of each gesture within the vocal tract.
